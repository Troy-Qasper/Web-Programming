New York, often referred to as the Empire State, is located in the northeastern United States. It shares borders with six other states: Pennsylvania and New Jersey to the south, Connecticut, Massachusetts, and Vermont to the east, and finally, Rhode Island to the southeast. To the north, it is bordered by the Canadian provinces of Ontario and Quebec. The state is defined by its iconic city, New York City, one of the world's major financial, cultural, and commercial centers.

With a population exceeding 19 million people, New York is the fourth most populous state in the U.S. The vast majority of its population resides in the New York metropolitan area, which includes not only New York City but also parts of New Jersey, Connecticut, and Pennsylvania.

New York's economy is highly diversified, with key industries including finance, media, technology, tourism, and manufacturing. Wall Street, located in Lower Manhattan, is the world's largest financial center. The state is also known for its contributions to the arts, fashion, and entertainment industries, with Broadway theater and the fashion district in Manhattan being internationally renowned.

In addition to its urban centers, New York is characterized by its natural beauty, including the scenic Adirondack and Catskill Mountains, the picturesque Finger Lakes region, and the stunning Niagara Falls on its western border with Canada.
